Campus Happenings

The first grade students are learning that books are not the only way to tell a story. They recently went on a field trip to the Scottsdale Museum of Contemporary Art where they learned that visual art can be a story telling medium as well. They also observed the differences between different artists as well as the common elements used such as line, shapes and space. The following week the 1st grade classes saw a performance by the Cookie Theater Company. The musical, which was based on a book called "Unstoppable Me", emphasized the fact that by doing your best you can attain your dreams.

The third grade classes went on a field trip to the Pioneer Living History Museum as part of their social studies unit on Arizona history. While there students participated in a scavenger hunt that took them around the grounds, including an old schoolhouse, a fort, a Victorian home, a print shop, a blacksmith and a miner's cabin.

Help Navajo with a Tax Credit Donation

Did you know that Arizona law allows taxpayers to receive a tax credit of up to $200 for individual filers and up to $400 for married filers for donations made to SUSD schools? You can claim the tax credit when filing your 2011 Arizona State Income Tax return. The school must receive the funds by December 31, 2011. It's a win, win situation for you and the school! Consult your tax professional.
