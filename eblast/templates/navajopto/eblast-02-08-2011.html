Navajo Flute Makers Hit the Right Note

"I want to take my flute home and play something for my Mom," said Araya Garcia. Several 5th grade gifted students have been studying the science of sound by exploring various musical instruments and their design. As a culminating activity, each student made a flute using Native American flute design principles. "It's like, wow! I made a musical instrument," commented Megan White."I found out how changing the size of the finger hole affects the pitch of the note."

Joe Grinis Patterson explained that, " ...the longer the tube, the lower the note." Students created small musical toys representing each family of instruments, woodwind, brass, string and percussion. Lydia Hans said that she now knows how to make reeds and how sound is amplified by the body of the instrument. But the flutes were the most exciting part of the experience. "My flute has a good sound," beamed Araya Garcia. Erik Alvarez Munoz explained his plans for the flute he made, "I listen to Navajo flute music at home," he said . "Now I'm going to learn how to play some of it."
