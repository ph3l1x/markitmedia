Science Night was a Blast!

Several hundred people attended Navajo's Annual Space and Science Night. Parents and students were able to view Jupiter, the moon and Orion's nebula on a beautiful clear night. They made and launched bottle rockets and learned about the constellations in a portable Star Lab. There were also live animals, including a great horned owl, red-tail hawk, snails and worms. Attendees also had the opportunity to learn about sound and pitch, circuits, and magnetism. The Smart Board demonstration highlighted the innovative use of technology in the classroom.

50th Anniversary Celebration

Over 400 people gathered at Navajo Saturday night to celebrate the school's 50th Anniversary. Alumni and former staff enjoyed a school tour highlighting the recent additions to the campus and facilities. Navajo Strings and Saguaro Strings performed in the main hallway. Afterwards, attendees were treated to a free dinner, donated in part by Marcella's Italian Kitchen, music and dancing. Alumni shared memories such as the current Computer Lab used to be Automotive Shop class and the Cafeteria used to be outside. The very first school secretary attended the event and shared that when school opened there were no working phones. The highlight for many alumni was the attendance of former principal Ed Fleming. The PTO would like to thank everyone who participated in the auction. The funds generated by your support are vital to PTO's extensive programs and activities.
