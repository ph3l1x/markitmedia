Navajo Receives AHA Grant

On Friday, March 11th, 10 new garden beds at Navajo Elementary were filled with nearly 300 seedlings! Navajo was 1 of 8 schools in the country that received a Teaching Gardens grant from the American Heart Association that promotes gardening as a way to teach children about nutrition and a healthy lifestyle. The grant provided cedar raised beds, soil, plants, seeds, gloves and trowels. Every student, from preschool through 6th grade, was able to participate in the planting of tomatoes, cucumbers, squash, eggplants, basil, peppers and strawberries. Earlier in the week, some of the 5th and 6th grade students assisted Mr. Loucks, Navajo's gifted teacher, in the assembly and filling of the raised beds as well as putting in the watering system.

Third Graders go to the IMAX

In March, the 3rd grade class went to a double feature at the IMAX Theater. They saw a 3D movie about the oceans and another about Jane Goodall's work with chimpanzees. These movies helped bring into focus the human impact on the environment, which is something students have been discussing in both science and social studies.
