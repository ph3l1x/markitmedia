3rd Grade Builds City of Scottsdale

Third graders learned all about city planning, land use, city layout and even some engineering. Students used LEGO blocks to build schools, hospitals, houses, parks, an airport, a firehouse, stores and theaters which were then assembled to represent the City of Scottsdale. Families were able to view the final project on April 14th.

Sixth Graders Investigate UFO Crash!

Students in grade six at Navajo Elementary have been working on a substantial project using Mindstorm NXT robots equipped with tiny video cameras. They are building towards a one-shot investigation using laptops, NXT software, and video grabbers, of an alleged UFO crash on Navajo's campus. They are grouped in teams with a Facilitator as lead, an engineer to build and maintain or modify the robot, a reporter to capture photos and real-time videos of the investigation, and a keyboarder who, on the actual day of the investigation, is in charge of real-time programming based on what the robot "sees" at the remote crash site, which has remained hidden. Final reports on each team's investigation will be created using EyePower Games' NewsMaker TV to create a "live" broadcast of the incident. Selected news broadcasts will be shared with Navajo's sister school in Singapore, Yu Neng Primary. Dr. Christine Loots (Technology) and Mr. Bret Loucks (Gifted) collaborated on the design and implementation of this real-life robotics project
